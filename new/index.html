What is CGPA and GPA?

GPA (Grade Point Average) is the average of the grades obtained by a student in a single semester or term. It is calculated by dividing the total grade points earned by the total credit hours attempted.

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the average of all GPAs earned by a student across all semesters or terms. It reflects the overall academic performance of a student throughout their program.

How to Calculate GPA and CGPA

GPA Calculation: \[ \text{GPA} = \frac{\text{Total Grade Points}}{\text{Total Credit Hours}} \] For example, if a student earns 45 grade points in 15 credit hours, their GPA is: \[ \text{GPA} = \frac{45}{15} = 3.0 \]

CGPA Calculation: \[ \text{CGPA} = \frac{\text{Sum of (GPA Ã— Credit Hours) for All Semesters}}{\text{Total Credit Hours Across All Semesters}} \] For example, if a student has a GPA of 3.5 in Semester 1 (15 credits) and 4.0 in Semester 2 (18 credits), their CGPA is: \[ \text{CGPA} = \frac{(3.5 \times 15) + (4.0 \times 18)}{15 + 18} = \frac{52.5 + 72}{33} = 3.77 \]

How CGPA and GPA Help Students

- Academic Progress Tracking: CGPA and GPA help students monitor their academic performance over time. - Scholarship Eligibility: Many scholarships require a minimum CGPA or GPA. - Graduation Requirements: Universities often set a minimum CGPA for graduation. - Career Opportunities: Employers and graduate schools often consider CGPA as a measure of a student's dedication and ability.
